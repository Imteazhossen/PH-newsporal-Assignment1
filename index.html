Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"





Innovation


Innovation is creating and applying new ideas, methods, or technologies to solve problems.



Sustainability


Sustainability is meeting present needs without compromising future generations' needs.



Infrastructure


Infrastructure is the fundamental systems and structures enabling society's operation.



Empowerment


Empowerment is giving individuals authority, resourc and to make decisions and take action.

The Vision of Future



Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.





Digital Transformation


Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.



Green Energy


Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.



Modern Infrastructure


Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.


Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Nobel Peace Prize laureate, is widely known for founding Grameen Bank and pioneering microcredit, which has transformed the lives of millions in Bangladesh by providing small loans to the poor, especially women. His work has revolutionized financial inclusion, helping people escape poverty through entrepreneurial ventures. Currently, Dr. Yunus continues to contribute to Bangladesh's development through his initiatives in social business, aiming to address social issues like health, education, and environmental sustainability. He advocates for a more inclusive economy and empowers the youth and women, fostering innovation and economic independence through sustainable, profit-driven social enterprises.
